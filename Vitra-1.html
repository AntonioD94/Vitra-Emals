      Introducing Abalon
           
Taking its name from the shell of a sea snail, Abalon forms its own architecture for seating circles: with its organically curved shape, the sofa evokes a circle to which people feel drawn. The expansive backrest has two functions: it shields sitters who are engaged in a private conversation and offers a sense of protection to those seeking retreat within an open environment.

Massive yet graceful
Abalon is unusual in appearance: solid yet light, massive yet graceful. It functions as a striking solo piece and when several sofas are used together, the concave and convex surfaces create a state of compelling tension, neither attracting nor repelling one other. The upholstered seat shell of the Abalon Sofa generously accommodates three people, while the curved shape creates a common focal point. The front and back of the seat shells are covered separately with different knitted fabrics, which can be chosen in various combinations. Together with the two available colours of the boldly defined base, this opens up a wide range of configuration options. The fabric covers of the sofa can be removed in full for cleaning or replacement, which further enhances its durability.

A perfect match
The Abalon Sofa can be complemented with Abalon Platform and Abalon Table. Abalon Platform is a round stool that can be used as a flexible seat as well as a footrest. The carefully proportioned, widely splayed legs of the base not only ensure a high degree of stability, but also clearly identify the stool as a member of the Abalon family. The Abalon Table is a circular coffee table, whose tops come in a choice of finishes.

Ronan & Erwan Bouroullec

Ronan and Erwan Bouroullec, born respectively in 1971 and 1976 in Quimper in France, studied at the cole Nationale des Arts Dcoratifs in Paris and at the cole des Beaux-Arts in Cergy-Pontoise. Ronan immediately began working as an independent designer after completing his studies, with the help of his brother, then still a student.

Since 1999 the brothers have been working together in their associated studio in Paris. Their creations range from small useful objects to architectural projects. In addition to the design of furniture for home and office environments, vases, porcelain tableware, jewelry and various home accessories, their work mainly focuses on the design and organization of interior spaces.
